G — Auditor Use Cases, Scope Boundaries & Non-Goals

Product positioning relative to Intuit and QBO; classification of auditor-requested capabilities; guardrails for backlog acceptance. Most auditor wish-list items are, in substance, a desire for the Audit Log to be extended and enriched — appropriate for Intuit's product, not for NGAME.

NGAME is a companion application, not a QuickBooks Online add-on. It runs on a separate surveillance workstation, reads company data through Intuit's read-only API, and presents its own dashboard. It does not install inside QBO, extend QBO screens, or replace QBO workflows. It is not marketed or licensed as a QBO marketplace extension.

NGAME is not an Audit Log substitute. Attribution ("who changed what, when"), void/delete audit trails, and authoritative field-level change history are Intuit's domain. NGAME deliberately defers those questions to human review of the QBO Audit Log (or auditor-built SQL over audit exports). Strategic intent: remain well separated from Audit Log functionality so NGAME is not viewed as a competitor to Intuit. NGAME's role is early statistical triage on aggregate churn patterns; the Audit Log remains the system of record for transactional forensics.

Platform note: No QBO Audit Log API exists (confirmed Intuit developer forums; June 2026).

Operational handoff (by design): Bookkeeper posts in QBO → NGAME (read-only API) emits category-level "unusual today" signals → FRP or auditor reviews QBO Audit Log in browser for who / what / when / field-level detail.

What NGAME is built to do: Almost stateless surveillance — primary memory is Yesterday.ttl vs Today.ttl; secondary memory is a 30-day training baseline (μ, σ per transaction type) and an optional rolling buffer of aggregate CPI arrays (~30 days). Alert voice is general (18 φ categories, not named entities). NGAME does not prove fraud; flags mean statistically unusual vs the trained baseline.

It does not list which records were deleted (doc #, amount, payee, who deleted) or rank alerts on deletion rate δ as a first-class FRP panel.
In scope (aggregate only): "More deletion churn than usual" via CPI z-scores. Out of scope: enumerating deleted transactions — functionally Audit Log enrichment.
