Abstract

[Draft — authors revise to ≤150 words per JFAR guidelines]

Small and medium enterprises and nonprofit organizations rely on cloud accounting platforms yet lack continuous forensic oversight between periodic audits. We present NGAME (Next-Generation Accounting Monitoring Engine), a design science artifact that extracts daily transaction data from QuickBooks Online, constructs RDF/OWL semantic snapshots, computes a 17–18 element Churn Persistence Index (CPI) phi array as a behavioral fingerprint, compares current-day activity against a trained baseline, and delivers Financially Responsible Person (FRP)–interpretable management warnings enriched by a locally hosted large language model aligned to an ACFE-based asset misappropriation ontology. Following the Design Science Research Methodology [Peffers et al., 2008; Geerts, 2011], we define solution objectives, describe artifact design and implementation, demonstrate operation on sandbox, injection, and live nonprofit deployments, and evaluate the artifact using Hevner et al.'s [2004] testing, analytical, observational, and descriptive evaluation categories. Evidence from April–May 2026 operational runs confirms end-to-end pipeline operability, convergent analytical signals on high-risk transaction-type dimensions, and FRP interpretability of outputs. Element-level precision and recall against injected ground truth are deferred pending test-harness instrumentation. The artifact addresses a structural gap in continuous SME surveillance not covered by episodic audit analytics tools.

Keywords: design science research; forensic accounting; fraud detection; accounting information systems; ontology; churn persistence index; QuickBooks Online; anomaly detection; large language model

§1 — Introduction (DSRM Activity 1 preview)

1  Introduction

Occupational fraud imposes disproportionate harm on small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and nonprofit organizations whose internal control environments depend on a single Financially Responsible Person (FRP)—typically an owner, board treasurer, or executive director who is not a trained accountant [ACFE, 2022]. The Association of Certified Fraud Examiners reports a median loss of $117,000 per scheme and a median duration of twelve months before detection when continuous monitoring is absent. Cloud accounting platforms such as QuickBooks Online (QBO) have made programmatic access to daily transaction data feasible, yet no commercially available tool provides automated, interpretable, continuous forensic oversight operable by a non-expert FRP without auditor intervention.

This paper presents NGAME, a design science artifact instantiated in Python that closes this gap through a four-layer analytical pipeline: (1) structural day-over-day record overlap measurement via the Churn Persistence Index; (2) statistical detection of anomalous transaction-type dimensions against a trained baseline; (3) scheme interpretation through taxonomy-grounded LLM enrichment; and (4) FRP adjudication of alerts as corroborated, benign on review, or missed. We position NGAME within the forensic and AIS design science literature and report evaluation evidence from controlled testing, analytical decomposition, nonprofit field deployment, and comparative argument.

The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section 2 reviews relevant literature. Sections 3–7 map sequentially to the six DSRM activities [Geerts, 2011]. Section 8 concludes with contribution statement and future work.

Research Questions

  • RQ1: Can daily transaction data from a cloud SME accounting platform be reliably represented as an RDF/OWL semantic snapshot suitable for persistent behavioral profiling?
  • RQ2: Can a multi-element CPI phi array derived from such snapshots provide a statistically defensible baseline for anomaly detection at the transaction-type dimension level?
  • RQ3: Can the full pipeline—extraction, ontology construction, CPI analysis, anomaly identification, misappropriation mapping, and management warning—be implemented as a deployable artifact operable by a non-technical FRP with locally hosted LLM inference?
§2 — Background (knowledge base for all DSRM activities)

2  Background and Literature Review

[Authors expand with full citations and forensic-accounting framing for JFAR audience]

2.1  Occupational Fraud and the FRP Oversight Gap

Forensic accounting research documents the economic significance of asset misappropriation and the organizational conditions under which fraud persists undetected [ACFE, 2022; Albrecht et al., 2015]. SMEs and nonprofits exhibit elevated vulnerability: segregation of duties is weak, the FRP often delegates bookkeeping, and periodic audit or bank reconciliation provides the primary detection mechanism—with median lags measured in months.

2.2  Audit Analytics and Continuous Monitoring

Established audit analytics platforms (ACL Analytics, IDEA) support post-period analytical procedures but presuppose trained auditor operators, flat-file or database exports, and episodic engagement [Bovee et al., 2005]. Continuous auditing research [Murthy & Groomer, 2004] established the conceptual foundation for automated exception identification but targeted enterprise ERP environments rather than cloud SME APIs.

2.3  Semantic Web and REA in AIS

The REA enterprise ontology tradition [Geerts & McCarthy, 2006; Sedbrook & Newmark, 2008] demonstrates that accounting semantics can be encoded in RDF/OWL for machine-interpretable analysis. NGAME repurposes this tradition toward fraud surveillance rather than enterprise system design—a repurposes link in the artifact network sense [Geerts, 2011].

2.4  Design Science Research in Accounting

Design science research produces and evaluates IT artifacts that solve identified organizational problems [Hevner et al., 2004; March & Smith, 1995]. Geerts [2011] adapts Peffers et al.'s [2008] six-activity DSRM for AIS journals. Recent guidance [Christ et al., 2026] emphasizes explicit artifact objectives, rigorous multi-method evaluation, and clear reporting—standards this paper adopts.

2.5  LLM-Assisted Forensic Analysis

Generative AI introduces interpretive capability but raises confidentiality and non-determinism concerns in financial settings. NGAME addresses these by deploying Ollama locally on the FRP's Surveillance Computer, bounding LLM output to Layer 3 scheme interpretation rather than autonomous fraud verdicts.

§3 — DSRM Activity 1: Problem Identification & Motivation

3  Problem Identification and Motivation

3.1  The Gap in SME Financial Oversight

The problem addressed is not merely fraud detection in the abstract but the absence of a continuous, FRP-operable surveillance instrument for cloud SME accounting data. Existing solutions require either auditor expertise (ACL/IDEA), platform-native reporting without statistical baselines (QBO/Wave dashboards), or enterprise-scale infrastructure unavailable to nonprofits and small businesses.

3.2  Weaknesses of Existing Solutions

No prior AIS artifact combines: (a) semantic ontology construction from cloud accounting REST/GraphQL APIs; (b) daily proportional behavioral profiling resilient to revenue scale changes; (c) ACFE-taxonomy-aligned scheme interpretation; and (d) local LLM enrichment without external data transmission—all operable by a non-accountant FRP.

3.3  Justification of Value

An FRP-deployable surveillance artifact addresses a documented, economically significant problem. The artifact sits at the intersection of forensic accounting, AIS, semantic web technologies, and locally hosted generative AI—a convergence timely for the Forensic Accounting Section research community. Practical relevance is demonstrated by live nonprofit deployment and planned university lab replication.

§4 — DSRM Activity 2: Define Objectives of a Solution

4  Objectives of a Solution

4.1  Solution Objectives (O1–O6)

Obj.ObjectiveSpecification
O1Semantic representationExtract daily posted transactions from QBO (OAuth REST) or Wave (GraphQL) and construct RDF/OWL TTL snapshots capturing chart-of-accounts and transaction-event semantics.
O2Churn profilingCompute a reproducible 17–18 element CPI phi array encoding proportional activity distribution across canonical transaction types each operational day.
O3Baseline constructionIn Training Mode, accumulate daily CPI vectors into a training matrix; derive per-element means and standard deviations (default N ≥ 30 days).
O4Anomaly detectionIn Fraud Analysis Mode, rank dimensions by |z-score| magnitude; surface top-k (default k = 3) anomalous transaction-type dimensions.
O5Misappropriation mappingMap flagged dimensions to ACFE-aligned asset misappropriation classes via Asset_Misappropriation.ttl and local Ollama RAG, auditable against a deterministic curated anchor.
O6FRP-accessible warningsProduce management-facing JSON and human-readable reports interpretable without accounting or technical expertise.

4.2  Design Constraints

  • All processing on the FRP's local Surveillance Computer; no server-side infrastructure beyond cloud accounting APIs.
  • Installable by a systems administrator; operable by the FRP via browser UI without command-line exposure.
  • LLM layer locally hosted (Ollama); no transaction data transmitted to external AI services.
  • Platform-agnostic Phase I–II pipeline supporting QBO and Wave via configuration.
§5 — DSRM Activity 3: Design and Development

5  Design and Development

5.1  Artifact Classification (March & Smith, 1995)

TypeNGAME instantiation
ConstructThe CPI phi array as a daily behavioral fingerprint of accounting activity.
ModelTwo-phase training/fraud-analysis pipeline; four-layer trust model (structural → detection → scheme interpretation → FRP adjudication).
MethodZ-score churn comparison; Jaccard-family day-over-day similarity; RAG-based LLM scheme interpretation with deterministic taxonomy anchor.
InstantiationWorking NGAME system (Python 3.10+, Flask UI), deployable on macOS/Windows, connecting to live QBO/Wave accounts.

5.2  Four-Layer Analytical Architecture

NGAME separates concerns across four layers, each with distinct vocabulary and trust properties [see NGAME_Glossary_Four_Layer_Terminology.md]:

LayerQuestionPrimary outputsTrust property
1 — StructuralHow stable is each transaction-type population day-over-day?CPI, α (addition rate), δ (deletion rate)Reproducibility
2 — DetectionWhich φ dimensions deviate enough to rank in top-k?z-scores, HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW bands, alert setDetection validity (eval. only)
3 — Scheme interpretationHow plausibly do patterns align with misappropriation scheme classes?Taxonomy mappings, LLM narrative, anchor scoresInterpretive fidelity
4 — FRP adjudicationWhat did human review conclude?Corroborated / benign / missed outcomesAdjudication outcome
Terminology discipline (critical for reviewers): Confusion-matrix terms (TP, FP, FN, TN, precision, recall) apply only to Layer 2 evaluation against known or injected ground truth. Layer 1 uses retained, added, removed, excluded—not TP/FP. Layer 4 benign resolutions are not detector false positives under NGAME's triage design.

5.3  Design Principles and Kernel Theory

  • Semantic representation: RDF/OWL enables SPARQL querying and fraud-taxonomy alignment [Geerts & McCarthy, 2006].
  • Interpretable associative metrics: Proportional phi distributions are robust to revenue scale and interpretable without domain expertise.
  • Baseline comparison: Z-score detection operationalizes expected-vs-actual at daily granularity [Murthy & Groomer, 2004].
  • Separation of concerns: Phase I (extraction/ontology) and Phase II (analysis/reporting) are independently testable.
  • Local inference: Ollama on the Surveillance Computer preserves confidentiality while enabling generative reasoning.

5.4  Phase I — Data Extraction and Ontology Construction

Data Extraction Agent authenticates via OAuth (QBO) or API key (Wave), extracts posted transactions, and writes structured JSON. Ontology Builder produces today-snapshot TTL aligned with the Curated Transaction Types ontology; yesterday's snapshot is retained for delta comparison. CPI Analysis Agent computes the phi array by mapping events to canonical transaction-type classes.

5.5  Phase II — Analysis, Detection, and Reporting

Training Mode (Matrix Management Agent) appends daily CPI rows and recalculates μ, σ. Fraud Analysis Mode executes Churn Comparison (z-scores), Anomaly Identification (top-k), Account Mapping, LLM Analysis (Layer 3), and Management Warning (FRP deliverables). Dual-mode entry (ngame_dual_mode.py) auto-selects mode based on training matrix row count.

5.6  Implementation Environment

Python 3.10+; rdflib, openpyxl, requests/gql, ollama; Git-versioned agent scripts; Flask UI (ngame_ui/).

§6 — DSRM Activity 4: Demonstration

6  Demonstration

6.1  Demonstration Contexts

  • QBO sandbox: End-to-end pipeline on Intuit sandbox company (Craig's Design and Landscaping).
  • Anomaly injection: Dedicated injection agent (ngame_anomaly_injection_agent.py) injects five ACFE-aligned pattern classes; full pipeline executed 25 April 2026.
  • Nonprofit field test: Live QBO production account; 30-day training window; fraud analysis mode activated April–May 2026.
  • University lab (planned): Multi-company academic deployment with controlled peer-introduced anomalies.

6.2  Illustrative Run — Live Operational Detection

On 28 April 2026, Fraud Analysis Mode against a mature training baseline flagged three of eighteen phi dimensions at HIGH deviation: Vendors (z = 5.0, +30.2% from mean), Contractors (+47.6%), and ChartOfAccounts (+102.2%). Independent day-over-day phi coefficient analysis showed sub-unity similarity on Vendors (0.863) and Contractors (0.855)—the only dimensions below perfect stability—converging with z-score rankings. The management dashboard delivered plain-language warnings reviewed and found interpretable by the FRP without technical explanation, satisfying O6.

The ChartOfAccounts alert traced to legitimate new GL accounts added by the bookkeeper—a benign resolution at Layer 4 demonstrating triage design: statistically warranted signal, FRP judgment, no missed event.

§7 — DSRM Activity 5: Evaluation

7  Evaluation

7.1  Evaluation Framework

Following Hevner et al. [2004] and Geerts [2011], this section applies testing, analytical, observational, and descriptive evaluation to NGAME, comparing observed performance against O1–O6. Experimental evaluation on genuine fraud is deferred (§7.8).

7.2  Metric Definitions (Layer 2 only)

Precision, recall, and false-positive rate apply solely to evaluation of top-k φ-dimension ranking against injected or documented ground truth—not to Layer 1 Jaccard overlap, Layer 3 scheme labels, or Layer 4 benign resolutions.

7.3  Testing Evaluation — Anomaly Injection Suite (25 Apr 2026)

Five pattern classes; 59 synthetic transactions ($1,695,096 synthetic value); 8/8 validation checks passed; pipeline confirmed operational end-to-end. Important: the harness records aggregate pass/fail, not per-pattern top-k rankings—element-level precision/recall cannot yet be computed from existing artifacts.

7.4  Analytical Evaluation — CPI Decomposition (28 Apr 2026)

Cosine similarity 0.9958; RMSE 0.0301; max |z| = 5.0 (Vendors); 15/18 dimensions at z = 0.0. Convergent validity: Vendors ranked #1 on a priori misappropriation risk score (0.878) independently of this run.

7.5  Observational Evaluation — Nonprofit Field Deployment

30-day training matrix accumulated on production QBO; fraud analysis completed; FRP found outputs interpretable; ChartOfAccounts benign resolution documented.

7.6  Descriptive Evaluation — Comparative Argument

NGAME vs. ACL/IDEA on deployment context, API access, automated baseline, taxonomy alignment, local LLM, FRP accessibility, and data confidentiality—see evaluation draft Table 5.4.

7.7  Convergent Validity Summary

Independent testing, z-score, phi-coefficient, a priori risk ranking, field deployment, and comparative argument converge on localized, theory-consistent alerting—not global noise.

7.8  Limitations and Future Evaluation Work

  1. No prospective controlled trial on genuine fraudulent data.
  2. Injection harness lacks per-pattern top-k instrumentation for formal precision/recall.
  3. Phi array taxonomy tied to QBO/Wave transaction types; other platforms unvalidated.
  4. LLM output variability not systematically characterized (grounding rate, anchor concordance, rerun stability deferred).
Retire outdated claims: The DSRM Outline canvas (ngame-dsrm-paper-outline.canvas.tsx) still references "83% top-3 recall" and "12 injected scenarios." These are not supported by current test artifacts. Do not publish precision/recall figures until the V1.1 evaluation harness (Category B in NGAME_V1_1_Backlog.html) instruments per-pattern rankings.

8  Conclusion and Communication

8.1  Contribution Statement

This paper contributes: (1) a new instantiation—NGAME—for continuous behavioral surveillance of SME cloud accounting data by non-expert FRPs; (2) a new method—the CPI phi array and four-layer trust model—as a fraud-sensitive daily behavioral fingerprint with disciplined evaluation vocabulary; (3) demonstration that locally hosted LLM scheme interpretation can enrich forensic triage without compromising data confidentiality. 8.3  Implications for Forensic Accounting Practice

NGAME compresses detection lag from months (median under no continuous monitoring) to same-business-day alerting for statistically anomalous transaction-type activity. It does not replace professional judgment, audit, or legal adjudication—it equips the FRP with a triage instrument aligned to ACFE scheme taxonomy.
