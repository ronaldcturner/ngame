Abstract

Responds to R1a, R1b, R2 Authors: Open with nonprofit + SME fraud statistic (ACFE 2022 nonprofit median loss/duration). State explicitly that this is a design science proof-of-concept at Technology Readiness Level 3 — pipeline demonstrated, formal detection metrics deferred. Do not cite “85% accuracy.”

Nonprofit organizations and small businesses delegate bookkeeping to staff or contractors while a volunteer or executive Financially Responsible Person (FRP) retains oversight—but often lacks tools for continuous forensic monitoring between audits. We present NGAME, a design science artifact that extracts daily QuickBooks Online transactions, builds RDF/OWL semantic snapshots, computes a multi-element Churn Persistence Index (CPI) behavioral fingerprint, compares activity against a trained organizational baseline, and delivers FRP-interpretable management warnings with locally hosted LLM scheme enrichment aligned to ACFE asset-misappropriation classes.

Following the Design Science Research Methodology [Peffers et al., 2008; Geerts, 2011; Myers et al., 2024], we report six sequential research activities: problem identification, solution objectives, design and development, demonstration, evaluation, and communication. Evaluation applies Hevner et al.'s [2004] testing, analytical, observational, and descriptive methods to evidence from sandbox validation, anomaly injection, a consenting nonprofit field deployment (April–May 2026), and FRP interpretability review. We position the artifact at TRL 3 (proof-of-concept demonstrated) and introduce a four-layer triage vocabulary that replaces undifferentiated false-positive/false-negative language. Element-level precision and recall against injected ground truth await test-harness instrumentation; we report convergent analytical validity and operational FRP usability instead.

Keywords: design science research; forensic accounting; nonprofit fraud; churn persistence index; QuickBooks Online; continuous monitoring; triage; accounting information systems

5.7  Training Baseline Design: Organizational Norm, Not Individual Bookkeeper Profiling

R7, R8 — Tara: training dependency & retraining Core argument to develop: The training matrix accumulates organization-level transaction-type distributions (φ vectors), not keystroke or user-attribution profiles. Any authorized bookkeeper whose posting patterns conform to the entity’s chart-of-accounts taxonomy contributes interchangeable rows. Turnover changes who posts, not the definition of Vendors or Bills. Retraining after turnover is the same 30-day Training Mode the FRP already runs—no new model architecture.

NGAME’s baseline is a vector of means (μ) and standard deviations (σ) computed per transaction-type dimension across N operational days (default N ≥ 30). Each training day captures the proportional distribution of activity classes—Vendors, Bills, BillPayments, and fourteen to sixteen additional canonical types—regardless of which user account created the underlying QBO records. The baseline therefore encodes organizational posting rhythm, not an individual bookkeeper’s typing habits, login times, or user ID.

When a bookkeeper leaves and a replacement begins posting, new daily rows continue to reflect the same entity’s chart-of-accounts structure. Transient stylistic differences (e.g., a new preparer batches invoices on Fridays instead of daily) appear as gradual baseline drift absorbed by rolling μ and σ—not as a requirement to rebuild the artifact from scratch. Only a structural change—new GL accounts, new service lines, merger—materially shifts multiple φ dimensions simultaneously, which is precisely the class of event NGAME is designed to surface for FRP review.

5.7.2  Retraining After Turnover or Role Change

R8 Describe the operational procedure in plain language: (1) FRP presses “Run Training Day” for 30 days after major org change, OR (2) consultant copies a fresh 30-day matrix from a parallel install. Emphasize built-in simplicity: dual-mode entry auto-switches; no code changes; FRP guide is one button per day.

Mitigation for bookkeeper turnover follows the same Training Mode already used at initial deployment. The FRP (or designated contact) executes daily training runs until the matrix again contains thirty day-columns; Fraud Analysis Mode re-engages automatically via ngame_dual_mode.py. No agent reconfiguration, ontology rebuild, or consultant visit is required unless the organization changes accounting platforms or materially restructures its chart of accounts. For organizations that experience seasonal posting patterns, the existing baseline literature recommendation—ensure the training window precedes the surveillance period—applies; NGAME does not introduce a novel retraining tax beyond that standard continuous-monitoring assumption.

7.2  Triage Vocabulary and Error-Rate Analysis

R9 — triage vocabulary (CPI vs wCPI) Tara’s task: Distill four-layer glossary into journal prose (~2 pages max). Table 7.1 below is the skeleton. Emphasize: CPI = presence/absence of record URIs (Layer 1); wCPI = same Jaccard structure dollar-weighted; neither uses FP/FN labels; Layer 2 alone uses precision/recall under controlled evaluation; Layer 4 uses benign resolution / corroborated alert.

Reviewers appropriately asked how NGAME treats false positives and false negatives. NGAME uses these terms in one restricted sense only—Layer 2 controlled evaluation—because undifferentiated confusion-matrix language misstates what earlier layers measure.

Layer 1 — CPI (presence–absence churn). For each transaction type, NGAME compares yesterday’s and today’s record sets. Cells of the 2×2 partition are retained (a), removed (b), and added (c)—not true/false positives. CPI = a/n is Jaccard-style set similarity describing structural churn. A legitimately new vendor is Added, not a “false positive.”

Layer 1 — wCPI (dollar-weighted churn). wCPI applies the same partition logic with dollar weights instead of URI counts: wCPI = Σ(retained dollars)/Σ(all churned dollars). wCPI answers “did large dollars move?” when record identities churn. wCPI is accumulated in the training matrix alongside count-based CPI; in the current proof-of-concept, count-based φ dimensions primarily drive the z-score alert vector, with wCPI providing a parallel dollar-sensitive channel for interpretation [authors: note A1 backlog item if wCPI not yet separate dashboard row].

Layer 2 — detection evaluation. Under injected or documented ground truth, standard precision, recall, and FPR apply to top-k transaction-type ranking—not to individual transactions or dollar amounts.

Layer 4 — FRP adjudication. An alert resolved as legitimate after FRP review (e.g., ChartOfAccounts spike from new GL accounts, April 28 2026) is a benign resolution—a statistically warranted signal correctly adjudicated—not a “false positive” evidencing broken detection.
