Jaccard Similarity — A Plain-English Guide

For accounting professionals. Illustrated with a real NGAME fraud-detection example.

What is Jaccard Similarity?

Jaccard similarity answers one plain question: out of everything we saw across two days, how much of it stayed the same?

You get a number between 0 and 1. A score of 1.0 means nothing changed overnight. A score of 0 means everything was replaced. In between, the number tells you exactly what fraction stayed stable.

NGAME Connection

In NGAME, Jaccard similarity is calculated for each of the 18 QuickBooks transaction types — Vendors, Invoices, Expenses, and so on — and stored as the Churn Persistence Index (CPI). A suspiciously low CPI for Vendors triggers a fraud investigation.

The Four-Compartment Box

Every record — an invoice ID, a vendor ID, etc. — falls into exactly one of four buckets depending on whether it appeared yesterday, today, or both. The rows ask about yesterday; the columns ask about today.

Record absent from both days. NGAME deliberately excludes this cell — you cannot count all the invoices that were never created.
Why is TN excluded from Jaccard?

The universe of things that never appeared in the books is boundless. There is no practical limit to the number of invoices, vendors, or expenses that could have existed but did not. Including that infinite denominator would collapse every score toward zero and make it meaningless. Jaccard wisely ignores TN entirely.

The Formula

Once you have your four buckets, the Jaccard score is just one division:

Jaccard (CPI)  =  TP ÷ (TP + FP + FN)
= Retained ÷ (Retained + Added + Deleted)
TN does not appear anywhere in the formula — it plays no role.
1.00
CPI = 1.0 — No change at all
0.50
CPI = 0.5 — Half the records turned over
0.00
CPI = 0.0 — Total replacement

Calculating the CPI

CPI = TP ÷ (TP + FP + FN)

CPI = 3 ÷ (3 + 2 + 2) = 3 ÷ 7 = 0.43

Only 43% of the combined vendor pool persisted unchanged overnight. Two established vendors vanished; two unknown vendors appeared from nowhere.

The baseline (trained over 30 normal business days) shows vendors almost never change — a CPI of 0.92 is typical. Today’s score of 0.43 is far below that. The z-score would be large and negative, placing Vendors at the top of NGAME’s anomaly ranking. The system maps the flagged Vendor accounts against the Asset Misappropriation taxonomy and issues a management-level warning for immediate review.

Why This Pattern Signals Fraud

What happened Box cell Fraud relevance
Vendor_001–003 stayed in the books TP — Retained Normal — long-standing suppliers. No concern.
Vendor_004–005 disappeared overnight FN — Deleted Possible concealment — removing legitimate supplier records to cover tracks.
Vendor_006–007 appeared overnight FP — Added Ghost vendors — fictitious suppliers inserted so fraudulent payments can be routed to them.
All other vendors in the universe TN — Excluded Not counted. Jaccard ignores this infinite category.

In a classic ghost-vendor scheme, a fraudster with QuickBooks access adds a vendor record under their control, approves a payment to it, then deletes the record to erase the evidence. NGAME’s daily CPI comparison detects both the addition and the deletion as deviations from the trained baseline — even if the record was deleted before the next morning’s manual review.

Reading Any CPI Score

CPI Score What it means in plain terms NGAME response
0.90 – 1.00 Almost nothing changed. Records are stable. No alert — normal operation
0.70 – 0.89 Some churn. A few adds or deletes. Low — monitor
0.43 – 0.69 Significant turnover. Many records changed. Medium — review accounts
0.00 – 0.42 Wholesale replacement. Records almost entirely replaced. HIGH — immediate investigation
The Z-Score Refinement

The raw CPI score alone does not trigger an alert. NGAME converts it to a z-score — how many standard deviations away from the 30-day trained average. This means NGAME adapts to each transaction type’s natural volatility. Some types churn more by nature; the z-score accounts for that and only flags deviations that are genuinely unusual for that specific account category.
